About me Research Teaching Other

I'm a music researcher who focuses primarily on computational methods for music analysis. The most interesting results for me are when my models misalign with my musical intuition; in my research, I continue to interrogate that misalignment, whether that's a bias of the model or a subjective priority of my own. I am an Assistant Professor of Music Theory at Baldwin Wallace University. Before BW, I taught at the University of Illinois, Union College, the Eastman School of Music (Ph.D), and Boston University (MM).

In my free time I like to take photos, bird watch, and play piano with my 4-hands partner (and partner) Eron.
