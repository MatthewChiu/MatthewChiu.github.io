About me

I'm a music researcher who focuses primarily on computational methods for music analysis. The most interesting results for me are when my models misalign with my musical intuition; in my research, I continue to interrogate that misalignment, whether that's a bias of the model or a subjective priority of my own. I'm a Ph.D. student in music theory at the Eastman School of Music, where I'm currently finishing my dissertation with Davy Temperley and serving as co-editor to Intégral.


In my free time I like to take photos, listen to music (in particular, Poulenc, Prokofiev, and DIY punk bands), and play piano with my 4-hands partner (and partner) Eron.
